# Yoga

## Overview
Yoga is a mind-body practice originating in ancient Indian philosophy that combines physical postures (asanas), breath control (pranayama), and meditation to promote flexibility, strength, autonomic balance, and psychological wellbeing. In cardiac rehabilitation, appropriately modified yoga is a Phase III-IV recommended modality for reducing sympathetic nervous system drive, improving heart rate variability, and supporting long-term stress management.

Not all yoga is safe for cardiac patients. Specific categories of postures and breathwork are contraindicated due to their hemodynamic effects.

## Key Facts
- **Modality type:** Mind-body practice; flexibility + neuromotor + autonomic regulation
- **Phase applicability:** Phase III-IV only (not appropriate in Phase I-II acute setting)
- **Evidence grade:** Moderate -- autonomic benefits well-documented; specific postures carry serious risk
- **Key distinction:** Style matters enormously. Restorative, Yin, and Chair Yoga are appropriate; Power/Hot/Vinyasa yoga is contraindicated.

## Contraindicated Categories for Cardiac Patients

### Inversions and Semi-Inversions
Headstand (Sirsasana), Shoulderstand (Sarvangasana), Plow Pose (Halasana), extended Downward-Facing Dog.
**Risk:** Gravity-induced venous return surge causes acute volume overload; carotid sinus compression triggers bradycardia/syncope.

### Deep Backbends
Wheel Pose (Chakrasana), Camel Pose (Ustrasana), Bow Pose (Dhanurasana).
**Risk:** Intense chest cavity pressure, elevated heart rate, dizziness, shortness of breath.

### Deep Torso Twists
Seated Spinal Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana), Revolved Triangle (Parivrtta Trikonasana).
**Risk:** Abdominal vessel compression causes erratic blood pressure shifts on pose release.

### Isometric Overhead Arm Holds
Warrior I with arms raised, Tree Pose held overhead.
**Risk:** Sustained isometric arm elevation increases systemic vascular resistance and afterload.

### Forceful Breathwork (Pranayama)
Kapalbhati, Bhastrika, any Kumbhaka (breath retention).
**Risk:** Mimics Valsalva maneuver; power/hot yoga causes dehydration and electrolyte imbalances leading to arrhythmia risk.

## Safe Yoga Practices for Cardiac Recovery

| Practice | Type | Cardiac Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Viparita Karani** (Legs-Up-The-Wall, supported) | Mild inversion | Lowers BP; reduces leg edema; no cardiac stress |
| **Supta Baddha Konasana** (Reclining Bound Angle, supported) | Restorative | Opens chest; enables full diaphragmatic breath |
| **Dirga Pranayama** (Three-Part Breathing) | Pranayama | Activates vagal tone; reduces HR and BP |
| **Nadi Shodhana** (Alternate Nostril Breathing) | Pranayama | Balances autonomic nervous system |
| **Cat-Cow** (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana) | Gentle movement | Maintains thoracic mobility; synchronized breath |
