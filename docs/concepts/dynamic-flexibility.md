# Dynamic Flexibility

## Definition / Explanation
Dynamic flexibility refers to the ability to move joints through a full range of motion with control and coordination. Unlike static stretching (holding a position), dynamic movement involves active muscle contractions that transition a joint through its functional path. This type of mobility is highly relevant for warming up and preparing the body for physical activity.

## Current State of Knowledge
Current research suggests that dynamic mobilization helps increase intra-muscular temperature and nervous system readiness without diminishing muscle power (a potential drawback of prolonged static stretching immediately before high-intensity training). For individuals focusing on long-term health and injury prevention, mastering these movement patterns is foundational for maintaining functional mobility.
