# **Optimizing Exercise Volume and Energy Expenditure Targets in CR**

* **Document Type:** Professional Position Statement

## **Executive Summary**

This document reviews the specific goals for the volume of aerobic exercise required to optimize long-term secondary prevention in cardiac rehabilitation, providing a methodology to calculate physical activity volume and comparing standard rehabilitation volumes with therapeutic prevention thresholds.

## **Quantifying Weekly Exercise Volume**

Volume is estimated by calculating MET-minutes per week ($\\text{MET-min/wk}$), derived by multiplying weekly frequency, session intensity in METs, and daily duration :

$$\\text{Volume (MET-min/wk)} \= \\text{Frequency (days/wk)} \\times \\text{Intensity (METs)} \\times \\text{Time (minutes/day)}$$  
Note: 1 MET represents resting metabolic oxygen expenditure and is defined as $1\\text{ MET} \= 3.5\\text{ mL}\\cdot\\text{kg}^{-1}\\cdot\\text{min}^{-1}$. An increase in functional capacity of just $1\\text{ mL}\\cdot\\text{kg}^{-1}\\cdot\\text{min}^{-1}$ is associated with a 10% reduction in cardiac mortality.

## **Energy Expenditure Targets: Clinical Thresholds vs. Reality**

* **The Guideline Target:** General health guidelines recommend a minimum of 150 minutes of moderate physical activity per week, which is equivalent to an energy expenditure of 1000 kcal/wk (or roughly 1050 MET-min/wk).  
* **The Rehabilitation Reality:** Traditional continuous aerobic exercise prescriptions (2 to 3 sessions per week for 30 to 45 minutes) often result in a modest energy expenditure of \<1000 kcal/wk.  
  * Younger male participants (\<65 years) average $305 \\pm 108\\text{ kcal}$ per session.  
  * Older male participants ($\\ge 65$ years) average $266 \\pm 100\\text{ kcal}$ per session.  
* **Secondary Prevention Thresholds:** Clinical research indicates that to halt the progression of coronary artery disease, a minimum physical activity threshold of 1500 kcal/wk is required. To achieve documented plaque regression, patients must achieve a mean volume of 2200 kcal/wk. Higher exercise volumes are also directly correlated with significantly greater improvements in cardiorespiratory fitness.
