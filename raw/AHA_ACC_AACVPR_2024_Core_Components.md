# **AHA/ACC/AACVPR 2024 Core Components of Cardiac Rehabilitation**

* **Source URL:** [https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001289](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001289)  
* **Document Type:** Clinical Joint Scientific Statement / Policy Guidelines

## **Executive Summary**

This document outlines the clinical framework and updates to the core components of secondary prevention in cardiac rehabilitation (CR), jointly published by the American Heart Association (AHA), American College of Cardiology (ACC), and the American Association of Cardiovascular and Pulmonary Rehabilitation (AACVPR). It establishes CR as a Class 1 therapeutic recommendation for patients recovering from myocardial infarction, CABG, stable angina, PCI, heart failure, and valve surgeries.

## **Key Updates in the 2024 Framework**

1. **Weight Management and Body Composition:** The component was expanded from basic weight/BMI assessment to mandate the evaluation of body composition, emphasizing the clinical measurement of lean muscle mass and fat distribution, as improvements in body composition are strongly linked to reduced cardiovascular risk.  
2. **Separation of Exercise Training:** Exercise training is now divided into two independent, standalone core components: Aerobic Exercise Training and Strength Training, reflecting the robust interval evidence base establishing the benefits of progressive resistance exercise.  
3. **Program Quality:** A new administrative component dedicated to tracking program-level outcomes (enrollment, adherence, and equitable access) to resolve historic underutilization and systemic barriers.

## **Patient Intake & Assessment Protocols**

Before starting outpatient exercise, clinical teams must complete a comprehensive intake assessment covering :

* **Cognitive and Physical Function:** Evaluation of station, gait, and frailty.  
* **Fall Risk and Balance Testing:** Postural tests are conducted to assess dynamic and static stability. Patients are assessed on their ability to stand with feet side-by-side, instep of one foot touching the big toe of the other, a tandem stance (heel-to-toe), and standing on one foot.  
* **Administrative and Clinical Records:** 12-lead ECG baseline telemetry, medication review, and home environment details (dwelling type, stairs, etc.).  
* **The Individualized Treatment Plan (ITP):** Every patient requires an ITP signed by a physician, formally updated every 30 days, with progress toward goals reviewed weekly by the clinical staff.
