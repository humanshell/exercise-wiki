# **Resistance Training Prescriptions and Acute Hemodynamic Responses**

* **Source URL:** [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8930531/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8930531/)  
* **Document Type:** Clinical Research and Comparative Review

## **Executive Summary**

This document establishes the scientific basis for incorporating resistance exercise (RE) into Phase II-IV cardiac rehabilitation. It contrasts general strength guidelines with cardiac-specific parameters and highlights critical comparative data regarding acute hemodynamic responses to varying training volumes.

## **Resistance Exercise Guidelines comparison**

| Guideline Body | Frequency | Intensity Targets | Volume and Structure | Equipment and Types |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **ACSM Outpatient CR** | 2-3 nonconsecutive days per week. | 40% to 60% of 1-RM; RPE 11 to 13 on the 6–20 scale. | 1 to 3 sets of 10 to 15 repetitions; 8 to 10 exercises. | Focus on major muscle groups; safe, comfortable equipment. |
| **AHA Cardiac Patients** | 2-3 nonconsecutive days per week. | Moderate, comfortable intensity. | 1 set of 10 to 15 repetitions; 8 to 10 exercises. | Isolated and compound movements. |

| **ACSM Muscular Endurance** | 2-3 nonconsecutive days per week. | Low-to-moderate intensity. | Multiple sets of 10 to 15 repetitions. | Varied modalities. |

## **Hemodynamic Responses: High Reps vs. High Loads**

A pivotal safety concern in cardiac resistance training is the acute rise in systemic blood pressure and heart rate, which increases rate pressure product ($RPP \= HR \\times SBP$) and myocardial oxygen demand.

A comparative study analyzed the hemodynamic impact of two distinct resistance training models :

1. **Low-Intensity, High-Repetition:** 4 sets of 17 repetitions at 40% of 1-RM.  
2. **High-Intensity, Low-Repetition:** 4 sets of 10 repetitions at 70% of 1-RM.

### **Key Clinical Finding**

While blood pressure rose with exercise intensity in both models, **significantly higher peak blood pressures were recorded during the high-repetition sets** (even at a lower percentage of 1-RM).

* **Clinical Translation:** Performing high repetitions to muscular failure causes cumulative local muscle fatigue and prolonged contractions that compress intramuscular blood vessels, resulting in an involuntary breath-hold and a dramatic surge in systemic vascular resistance. Therefore, to prevent dangerous blood pressure spikes, cardiac rehabilitation patients should perform brief, non-fatiguing sets (e.g., 5 to 10 repetitions) at light-to-moderate loads ($\<30\\%\\text{ to }40\\%\\text{ 1-RM}$) and strictly avoid training to muscular failure.
