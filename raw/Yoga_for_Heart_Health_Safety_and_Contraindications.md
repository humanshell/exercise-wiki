# **Yoga Safety Guidelines, Contraindicated Postures, and Heart-Safe Alternatives**

* **Source URL:** [https://www.apollo247.com/health-topics/heart-disease/yoga-for-heart-health-poses-to-avoid-and-safe-alternatives](https://www.apollo247.com/health-topics/heart-disease/yoga-for-heart-health-poses-to-avoid-and-safe-alternatives) & [https://psrihospital.com/which-asanas-to-avoid-if-suffering-from-heart-disease/](https://psrihospital.com/which-asanas-to-avoid-if-suffering-from-heart-disease/)  
* **Document Type:** Patient Safety Protocol & Specialty Clinical Guidance

## **Executive Summary**

Yoga is highly effective for reducing stress and lowering sympathetic nervous system drive in recovering cardiac patients. However, specific postures can alter hemodynamics, spike blood pressure, increase intrathoracic pressure, or cause vagal disturbances, making them dangerous for individuals recovering from a cardiac event.

## **Contraindicated Postures and Practices**

### **1\. Inversions and Semi-Inversions**

* **Poses to Avoid:** Headstand (*Sirsasana*), Shoulderstand (*Sarvangasana*), Plow Pose (*Halasana*), and Downward-Facing Dog.  
* **Physiological Risk:** Placing the heart below the lower extremities triggers a gravity-induced surge in venous return, causing acute volume overload that forces the heart to work harder. These poses also cause rapid elevations in blood pressure and intracranial pressure. Neck-compressing poses (e.g., Shoulderstand) put pressure on the carotid sinus, which can trigger severe bradycardia or syncope by disrupting blood pressure regulation feedback loops.

### **2\. Deep Backbends and Heart Openers**

* **Poses to Avoid:** Wheel Pose (*Chakrasana*), Camel Pose (*Ustrasana*), and Bow Pose (*Dhanurasana*).  
* **Physiological Risk:** These deep, unsupported positions are highly stimulating. They generate intense pressure in the chest cavity, elevate the heart rate, and can cause dizziness or shortness of breath.

### **3\. Intense Torso Twists**

* **Poses to Avoid:** Seated Spinal Twist (*Ardha Matsyendrasana*) and Revolved Triangle Pose (*Parivrtta Trikonasana*).  
* **Physiological Risk:** Deep abdominal twisting compresses major blood vessels in the chest and abdomen, restricting local circulation and leading to sudden, erratic shifts in blood pressure when the pose is released.

### **4\. Isometric Overhead Arm Poses**

* **Poses to Avoid:** Warrior I (*Virabhadrasana I*) and Tree Pose (*Vrikshasana*) held with arms overhead.  
* **Physiological Risk:** Keeping the arms above the head for extended periods increases systemic peripheral resistance and raises blood pressure, putting extra strain on the heart.

### **5\. Forceful Breathwork (Pranayama) and Sequences**

* **Techniques to Avoid:** Power/Hot Yoga sequences, any form of voluntary breath retention (*Kumbhaka*), and rapid, forceful breathing exercises like *Kapalbhati* (Skull-Shining Breath) and *Bhastrika* (Bellows Breath).  
* **Physiological Risk:** Power/Hot sequences cause dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, potentially triggering arrhythmias. Breath retention alters blood chemistry and pressure, mimicking the Valsalva maneuver, while forceful breathing increases thoracic pressure and cardiac strain.

## **Safe, Heart-Restorative Alternatives**

Recovery programs should focus on gentle, supported styles such as Restorative, Yin, or Chair Yoga :

* **Supported Legs-Up-The-Wall Pose (*Viparita Karani*):** Provides the circulatory benefits of a mild inversion (assisting venous return, reducing leg edema, and lowering blood pressure) without placing stress on the heart.  
* **Supported Reclining Bound Angle (*Supta Baddha Konasana*):** Opens the chest gently using bolsters and blocks, allowing deep diaphragmatic breathing without physical strain.  
* **Mindful Breathwork:** Slow, deep diaphragmatic breathing (*Dirga Pranayama*) and alternate nostril breathing (*Nadi Shodhana*) are highly recommended to calm the nervous system and regulate heart rate.
