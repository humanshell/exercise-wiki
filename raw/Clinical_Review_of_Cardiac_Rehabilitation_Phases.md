# **The Multi-Phase Clinical Framework of Cardiac Rehabilitation**

* **Source URL:** [https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK537196/](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK537196/) & [https://westgatehillshc.com/resources/4-cardiac-rehab-phases/](https://westgatehillshc.com/resources/4-cardiac-rehab-phases/)  
* **Document Type:** Clinical Reference Manual

## **Executive Summary**

Cardiac rehabilitation is a structured, interprofessional intervention designed to reduce physiological and psychological stress, improve cardiovascular function, and lower mortality risk. Recovery is divided into four distinct clinical phases that transition a patient from acute post-event hospitalization to independent, lifelong wellness.

## **The Four Phases of Rehabilitation**

### **Phase I: Acute Inpatient**

* **Timeline:** Initiated in the hospital immediately following stabilization after a cardiac event or surgical procedure; typically lasts a few days.  
* **Primary Goals:** Prevent hospital-related physical deconditioning, assess initial cardiovascular response to basic mobility, and complete a safe discharge plan.  
* **Activities:** Passive range-of-motion, assisted sitting and standing, deep diaphragmatic breathing, and short-hallway walks.  
* **Clinical Monitoring:** Intensive medical supervision, including continuous ECG telemetry, blood pressure checks, oxygen saturation, and cardiac enzyme tracking.

### **Phase II: Early Outpatient (Subacute)**

* **Timeline:** Commences within 1 to 2 weeks of hospital discharge; typically spans 3 to 12 weeks, with insurance commonly covering 36 sessions (3 times per week for 12 weeks).  
* **Primary Goals:** Safely increase endurance and strength under medical supervision, manage cardiovascular risk factors, and build heart-healthy lifestyle routines.  
* **Activities:** Treadmill walking, recumbent stationary biking, light resistance band training, and flexibility work.  
* **Clinical Monitoring:** Continuous 12-lead ECG telemetry during physical exercise, alongside regular blood pressure, oxygen, and resting blood glucose checks.

### **Phase III: Intensive Outpatient**

* **Timeline:** Typically spans 6 to 12 weeks following Phase II.  
* **Primary Goals:** Maximize aerobic capacity and muscle strength while transitioning the patient toward independent self-monitoring.  
* **Activities:** Progressively longer walking intervals, rowing machines, elliptical trainers, stair stepping, and full-body circuit training.  
* **Clinical Monitoring:** Shift from continuous telemetry to intermittent spot-checks, with the patient actively tracking their own heart rate and perceived exertion.

### **Phase IV: Long-Term Maintenance**

* **Timeline:** Ongoing, lifelong self-managed phase.  
* **Primary Goals:** Sustain cardiorespiratory fitness, prevent the recurrence of cardiac events, and reinforce healthy nutritional and behavioral habits.  
* **Activities:** Community walking groups, lap swimming, outdoor cycling, home-based strength maintenance workouts, and restorative yoga.  
* **Clinical Monitoring:** Unsupervised self-monitoring of heart rate and RPE, supported by periodic outpatient follow-up visits with cardiologists.
