# **Outpatient Exercise Intensity, Prescription, and Progression Standards**

* **Source URL:** [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8938824/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8938824/)  
* **Document Type:** Clinical Practice Research Analysis

## **Executive Summary**

This source analyzes the real-world execution of safe and progressive exercise prescriptions across 36 sessions of Phase II cardiac rehabilitation, highlighting the difference between prescriptions guided by a Graded Exercise Test (GXT) and those established in its absence.

## **Target Training Intensity Benchmarks**

* **Aero-Capacity Standard:** The optimal therapeutic target is moderate-to-vigorous intensity, clinically defined as 40% to 80% of heart rate reserve (HRR) or oxygen consumption reserve ($\\dot{V}\\text{O}\_2\\text{R}$).  
* **The GXT Gap:** While guidelines strongly encourage an intake GXT to establish a precise heart rate and functional baseline, data shows only 30% of cardiac rehabilitation clinics actually perform intake stress testing.  
* **Prescribing in the Absence of a GXT:** In the absence of intake GXT data, initial training targets must be estimated using the resting heart rate plus 20 to 30 beats per minute ($HR\_{\\text{rest}} \+ 20\\text{ to }30\\text{ bpm}$) alongside a strict subjective ceiling using a Borg Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE) of 11 to 14 ("light" to "somewhat hard" on a 20-point scale).

## **Clinical Rules for Exercise Progression**

* **Duration-First Sequencing:** Progression must prioritize increasing session duration before adjusting speed or resistance load. Among responding clinics, 60 out of 77 explicitly prioritize "duration first" progression, while only 10 adjust "intensity first".  
* **Ranked Progression Indicators:** When deciding to progress a patient's workload, clinicians rely on three key clinical metrics in ranked order :  
  1. Sustained subjective RPE (ensuring the patient finds the current workload comfortable).  
  2. Sustained, stable hemodynamic response (blood pressure and ECG telemetry).  
  3. Completion of a predetermined number of sessions.  
* **Documentation Frequency:** Progression adjustments must be systematically recorded. 59 out of 73 surveyed clinics document progression per session, while 13 do so weekly.
