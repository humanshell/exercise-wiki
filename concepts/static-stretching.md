# Static Stretching

## Definition / Explanation
Static stretching involves moving a joint through its full range of motion and then performing a stretch by holding that position for a period of time (typically 15 to 60 seconds). It is primarily used to increase muscle-tendon unit length and improve long-term flexibility.

## Current State of Knowledge
While widely practiced, current research suggests that static stretching may have different impacts depending on when it is performed. Performing lengthy static stretches immediately before high-intensity strength training can potentially lead to a temporary decrease in power output. However, when used as part of a cool-down or as a standalone flexibility session, it is an effective tool for improving range of motion and promoting relaxation.
